EW Planning Calculator

Determines whether your jammer has enough power to overpower the enemy radio at the target location. Fill in each section top to bottom — results update automatically.

  1. EA Platform — Enter your jammer output in Watts or dBm, then add your antenna gain. EIRP is calculated automatically.
  2. Frequency Hopping Tax — Check this box only if the enemy radio uses frequency hopping and your jammer cannot follow the hop pattern. Enter both bandwidths in kHz.
  3. EA Path Loss — Enter the distance from your jammer to the target, the operating frequency in MHz, and the terrain type. The calculator selects the correct path loss model automatically (<1 km or ≥1 km).
  4. Enemy Radio — Enter the enemy radio's power and antenna gain to calculate its EIRP.
  5. Enemy Radio Path Loss — Enter the distance between the enemy transmitter and receiver, their frequency, and terrain type.
  6. Jamming Assessment — Compares received jamming power against received enemy signal strength. A margin of +6 dB or greater means complete jamming.

Estimates the maximum range at which your sensor can detect an enemy emission. Fill in each section top to bottom — results update automatically.

  1. Enemy Emitter — Enter the enemy emitter's power in Watts or dBm and its antenna gain. Enemy EIRP is calculated automatically.
  2. Sensor Capabilities — Enter your sensor's antenna gain and equipment sensitivity (the minimum signal level your receiver can detect, typically a negative dBm value). Max Loss is calculated automatically.
  3. Detection Range — Enter the enemy's frequency in MHz and select the terrain type. The estimated detection range is shown in kilometers.
